# Example: Desert Hope Community Resource Center
## Complete Nonprofit & Fundraising Marketing Kit

**Organization:** Desert Hope Community Resource Center  
**Location:** Henderson, NV (serves Henderson + North Las Vegas)  
**Mission:** Provide food security, emergency housing assistance, and workforce development to families in poverty across Southern Nevada.  
**Annual budget:** $2.1M  
**Programs:** Summer Meals (children), Emergency Housing Navigation, Job Readiness Training

## Prompt 1 Output — Donor Appeal Letter Suite (Summer Campaign)

### Acquisition Letter (excerpt)

**[DESERT HOPE LETTERHEAD]**

Dear Friend,

Last June, Marcus Torres almost didn't get to eat.

His mom, Elena, worked split shifts at a casino downtown. She couldn't get him to our summer meals site before her shift started — and Marcus, quiet, a little too thin for a rising 6th grader, told our volunteer coordinator he'd "probably just stay home."

Rosa, our volunteer coordinator, arranged a pickup route. She drove six blocks out of her way, twice a week, all summer.

Marcus ate 72 meals that summer. When he started school in August, his teacher called Elena. "I don't know what happened this summer," she said. "But Marcus is different. He's focused."

What happened was $35.

Here in Henderson, 1 in 5 children faces food insecurity during summer — when the school cafeteria closes and the heat index hits 112°F. We served 1,247 children last summer. We need to serve more this June.

**Your gift of $35 provides 5 meals.**  
**Your gift of $75 provides 10.**  
**Your gift of $150 provides one child with three full weeks.**

Will you help us feed the next Marcus?

Warmly,

James Calderon  
Executive Director, Desert Hope Community Resource Center

## Prompt 2 Output — Volunteer Recruitment Campaign (excerpt)

### Volunteer Role Description: Meals Site Volunteer

**Meals Site Volunteer — Henderson NV, 3 hrs/week (Monday/Wednesday/Friday, 11AM-2PM, June-August)**

For three hours on a summer weekday, you can make sure a child in Henderson eats.

At Desert Hope's summer meals sites, volunteers receive shipments, set up serving stations, and spend two hours handing out hot lunches to children who depend on this program when school is out. Many of our kids come back every day — you'll know their names by Week 2.

**What you'll do:** Unload and sort food deliveries, set up serving tables, serve meals to children and families, assist with cleanup and site breakdown.

**Who thrives here:** People who like showing up, like kids, and don't mind working in a warm church kitchen. No experience required. We'll train you on food safety.

**What you'll gain:** Real relationships with the children and families you serve, food handler training (we cover it), and the knowledge that your Tuesday morning kept a kid focused in school through August.

[APPLY AT deserthopehenderson.org/volunteer]

### Corporate Volunteer Day Pitch (excerpt)

**Desert Hope Community Resource Center × [COMPANY NAME] — Community Partnership Opportunity**

---

**About Desert Hope**

For 23 years, Desert Hope has provided food security, emergency housing navigation, and workforce training to families in Henderson and North Las Vegas. Last year, we served 8,400 families — with 340 volunteers who collectively logged 14,200 hours.

**The Opportunity**

A corporate volunteer day at Desert Hope runs 3-4 hours on a weekday morning or weekend. Your team will sort and distribute food at our Henderson distribution center — hands-on, physical, and immediately impactful. In a 3-hour morning, a team of 20 can sort and package food for 200-300 families.

**Why Your Team Will Love It**
- No experience required — we train on arrival
- Meaningful work in the first 15 minutes (no orientation marathons)

**The Ask**

We're looking for a corporate volunteer day with 15-25 employees between June and August — our peak summer meals season when the need is highest and hands matter most.

## Prompt 3 Output — Annual Report Narrative (excerpt)

### Opening Narrative

This year, 1,247 children in Henderson ate a hot meal on a Tuesday in July.

That sounds like an ordinary thing. In the neighborhoods we serve — where summer means no school cafeteria, where a parent might be working two jobs and still coming up short on rent — it wasn't ordinary at all. It was the difference between a child who went back to school in August ready to learn, and one who didn't.

For 23 years, Desert Hope has filled the gaps that government programs leave behind. Last year, we served 8,400 families across Henderson and North Las Vegas — more than any year in our history. We did it with 340 volunteers, 42 staff members, and the generosity of 3,100 donors who believe that a neighbor in need is a community's responsibility.

Eighty-two cents of every dollar you gave went directly to programs. Not to overhead. Not to administration. To Marcus and Elena and Rosa and the 8,399 other families we served.

The need has not gone away. Read on for the story of what we did together — and what we're going to do next.

## Prompt 4 Output — Fundraising Event Kit (excerpt)

### Fund-a-Need Appeal Script — Beat 3: The Ask

[EMCEE AT PODIUM. HOUSE LIGHTS SLIGHTLY DOWN. ROOM IS QUIET.]

I want to ask you to do something right now.

[PAUSE — 3 seconds]

Take out your paddle. It's on your chair, or your table. Pick it up.

[PAUSE]

I'm going to tell you what happens when you raise it.

[PAUSE]

**Five hundred dollars.** That's one child. One summer. Sixty meals. Marcus Torres — the boy we just heard about — his summer cost $500. If you raise your paddle for $500 tonight, you are Marcus's summer.

[PAUSE. WAIT. LOOK AROUND THE ROOM.]

I need 10 people at $500. Who will be first?

[WAIT. MAKE EYE CONTACT. ACKNOWLEDGE FIRST PADDLE — "THANK YOU. TABLE 7. THAT'S ONE CHILD."]

Who's second? [PAUSE] Table 4 — that's two children.

[CONTINUE UNTIL 10 PADDLES OR MOMENTUM STALLS]

---

**Two hundred and fifty dollars.** Half a summer. We'll find someone to cover the other half. I need 20 people at $250.

[SAME PATTERN]

---

**One hundred dollars.** Twelve meals. Two weeks of Tuesdays for one child.

Everyone in this room can give $100. Your paddle is in your hand.

[PAUSE — LONGER THIS TIME]

If you've already given tonight — thank you. You can put your paddle down.

If you haven't — raise it now.

[PAUSE. COUNT. ACKNOWLEDGE. THANK.]

---

[ONCE COUNTING IS COMPLETE:]

We raised $[ANNOUNCE TOTAL] tonight for the Summer Meals Program.

[PAUSE. LET IT LAND.]

That's [NUMBER] children who will eat this summer because you were in this room tonight.

Thank you.

[TRANSITION TO NEXT PROGRAM ELEMENT]

---

*Note: Practice this script aloud twice. Time it. The pauses are where the money comes from — don't rush them. Have a volunteer with a hand counter tracking pledges in real time so you can announce the total immediately.*
