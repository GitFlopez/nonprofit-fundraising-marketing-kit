# Prompt 2 — Volunteer Recruitment Campaign

## Use This When
You need to recruit volunteers — whether for a one-time event, an ongoing program, or a corporate volunteer day. Also use this to retain and re-engage existing volunteers.

## What You'll Get
- 3 volunteer opportunity descriptions (150 words each, ready for VolunteerMatch / website)
- Cold recruitment email (individual + corporate versions)
- Social media kit: 4 posts per platform (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)
- 3-email volunteer retention/re-engagement sequence
- FAQ page copy for your website's volunteer section
- Corporate volunteer program pitch (1-page proposal for employers)

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert nonprofit volunteer coordinator and communications specialist. Write a complete volunteer recruitment and retention campaign using these inputs:

**ORGANIZATION INFO:**
- Name: [ORGANIZATION NAME]
- Mission (1 sentence): [MISSION]
- Location / service area: [CITY/REGION]

**VOLUNTEER ROLES (provide 3):**

Role 1:
- Title: [ROLE TITLE]
- Commitment: [e.g., "4 hours/month, Saturday mornings"]
- Key tasks: [3-4 bullet points]
- Skills needed: [e.g., "no experience required" or "must have valid driver's license"]
- Impact: [what this volunteer makes possible]

Role 2:
- Title: [ROLE TITLE]
- Commitment: [TIME COMMITMENT]
- Key tasks: [3-4 bullet points]
- Skills needed: [SKILLS]
- Impact: [IMPACT]

Role 3:
- Title: [ROLE TITLE]
- Commitment: [TIME COMMITMENT]
- Key tasks: [3-4 bullet points]
- Skills needed: [SKILLS]
- Impact: [IMPACT]

**IMPACT DATA:**
- Total volunteers last year: [NUMBER]
- Hours served: [NUMBER]
- Outcome enabled by volunteers: [e.g., "volunteers served 45,000 meals to 3,200 families"]

**CONTACT:**
- Volunteer coordinator name and email: [NAME, EMAIL]
- Application link or sign-up URL: [URL OR "TBD"]

**TARGET CORPORATE EMPLOYER (for corporate pitch — optional):**
- Company name: [NAME OR "generic"]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Employee count: [APPROX NUMBER]

---

DELIVER:

## SECTION 1: VOLUNTEER OPPORTUNITY DESCRIPTIONS (3 roles)

For each role, write a 150-word description in this format:
- Headline: [ROLE TITLE] — [LOCATION OR REMOTE], [TIME COMMITMENT]
- Opening hook: why this role matters (1-2 sentences)
- What you'll do: 4 specific tasks (bullets)
- Who thrives in this role: 2-3 traits or background types
- What you'll gain: skills, connections, community (genuine, not transactional)
- CTA: "[SIGN UP LINK / APPLY TODAY]"

These should be ready to paste into VolunteerMatch, Idealist, or your website volunteer page.

---

## SECTION 2: RECRUITMENT EMAIL (INDIVIDUAL VERSION)

Subject line: [3 options]

Cold email to someone who expressed interest or attended an event:
- Length: 200-250 words
- Structure: personal opening → mission moment → specific ask (role + time) → easy next step
- Include: application link and coordinator contact
- Tone: warm, specific, non-pressuring

---

## SECTION 3: RECRUITMENT EMAIL (CORPORATE VERSION)

Subject line: [3 options]

Email to HR director or CSR manager at a local company:
- Length: 300-350 words
- Structure: credibility hook → community connection → what a corporate volunteer day looks like → ROI for the company (team building, CSR reporting, employee satisfaction) → specific ask
- Include: number of employees that can participate, what date flexibility looks like, coordinator contact
- Frame it as a business case, not a charity ask

---

## SECTION 4: SOCIAL MEDIA KIT

Write 4 posts per platform, ready to schedule 1 per week:

**FACEBOOK (each post 100-150 words + image brief):**
- Post 1: Mission + impact story (awareness)
- Post 2: Specific role spotlight (recruitment)
- Post 3: Volunteer testimonial / quote format (social proof)
- Post 4: Event or deadline urgency (conversion)

**INSTAGRAM (each post: caption 100-125 words + 3-5 hashtags + image brief):**
- Post 1: Behind-the-scenes volunteer moment (authentic)
- Post 2: Role spotlight with application CTA
- Post 3: Impact stat formatted as bold graphic copy
- Post 4: "A day in the life of a [ROLE TITLE]" (story format)

**LINKEDIN (each post 150-200 words — thought leadership tone):**
- Post 1: Why [ORGANIZATION] needs people with professional skills (not just extra time)
- Post 2: Corporate volunteer day pitch to employers (tag HR/CSR professionals)
- Post 3: Volunteer impact stat + connection to professional development
- Post 4: "We're looking for [SKILL TYPE] volunteers" — direct recruitment

**HASHTAGS FOR ALL PLATFORMS:**
Generate 15 relevant hashtags for this nonprofit's volunteer campaign (mix of broad + niche + local).

---

## SECTION 5: VOLUNTEER RETENTION SEQUENCE (3 emails)

**Email 1 — Post-Shift Thank You (send within 24 hours):**
- Subject: [3 options]
- Length: 150-200 words
- Content: specific acknowledgment of what they did → impact their shift had → one sentence invite to return
- Tone: genuine, brief, personal

**Email 2 — 30-Day Check-In (send 30 days after first shift):**
- Subject: [3 options]
- Length: 200-250 words
- Content: how they're doing + what's changed at the org + invite to next shift + new opportunity spotlight
- Include: easy way to update availability or preferences

**Email 3 — Re-Engagement (send to volunteers who haven't returned in 90 days):**
- Subject: [3 options]
- Length: 200-250 words
- Content: "We miss you" framing + what's happened since they last volunteered + low-barrier re-engagement offer (shorter time commitment, different role, virtual option)
- No guilt — focus on their value, not their absence

---

## SECTION 6: WEBSITE FAQ COPY

Write 8 Q&As for a "Volunteer With Us" FAQ section:
1. Do I need experience to volunteer?
2. What's the time commitment?
3. Can I volunteer as a group or with coworkers?
4. Is there an age requirement?
5. What should I wear / bring?
6. Can I volunteer remotely?
7. How do I sign up?
8. What happens after I submit my application?

Format: Question in H3, answer 50-75 words. Conversational, specific to the organization.

---

## SECTION 7: CORPORATE VOLUNTEER PROGRAM PITCH (1 PAGE)

Header: [ORGANIZATION NAME] × [COMPANY NAME] — Community Partnership

Sections:
1. **About [Organization]** (2-3 sentences, mission + scale)
2. **The Opportunity** (what a corporate volunteer day looks like: activities, format, duration)
3. **Why Your Team Will Love It** (3 bullets: team building, community connection, skill application)
4. **Why It Matters for [Company]** (CSR reporting, employee satisfaction data — quote if available, or use industry stats: "93% of employees who volunteer through work report higher job satisfaction" — cite if used)
5. **The Ask** (specific: "We're looking for a [Month/Quarter] volunteer day with 15-25 employees")
6. **Next Step** (coordinator contact + calendar link)

Length: 350-400 words. Formatted as a one-pager ready to attach to email.
```

## Example Output (Excerpt)

**Organization:** Three Bridges Youth & Family Services, Henderson NV  
**Roles:** Homework Help Tutor / Food Pantry Sorter / After-School Program Assistant

---

**Volunteer Opportunity Description — Homework Help Tutor:**

> **Homework Help Tutor — Henderson NV, 2 hrs/week (Tuesdays or Thursdays, 3:30-5:30 PM)**
>
> Thirty minutes of focused help can change a student's entire week.
>
> At Three Bridges, our Homework Help volunteers sit one-on-one with K-8th graders who need a patient, encouraging presence to get through the day's assignments. Many of our students are English language learners; some come to us from unstable housing situations. You don't need to be a teacher — you need to care and show up.
>
> **What you'll do:** Review daily assignments with 1-2 students, provide encouragement and clarification, flag learning gaps to our program coordinator, and celebrate wins.
>
> **Who thrives here:** Retired educators, college students in education or social work, parents who love mentoring, or anyone who remembers a teacher who made a difference.
>
> **What you'll gain:** Real mentoring experience, a direct relationship with a student, and the knowledge that your Tuesday afternoons changed something.
>
> [Apply at threebriges.org/volunteer]

---

**Corporate Pitch — Opening:**

> When 22 employees from Desert Sun Realty volunteered at Three Bridges last October, they sorted and distributed food for 340 families in 3 hours. By 1 PM, the entire team was having lunch together — and four of them signed up to come back monthly on their own time.
>
> That's what a corporate volunteer day with Three Bridges looks like: purposeful, hands-on, and genuinely fun.

---

## Pro Tips

- **The corporate pitch is a business case, not a charity ask.** Lead with what the company gets (team morale, CSR reporting, employee retention data). The mission is the context, not the hook.
- **Volunteer retention beats recruitment every time.** One volunteer who comes back 12 times is worth more than 12 one-time volunteers. Send the 24-hour thank-you email without fail.
- **Facebook performs for 35+ volunteers; Instagram for 25-34; LinkedIn for corporate.** Post the same content differently across platforms.
- **The FAQ page is a conversion tool.** Every question on that page is an objection someone has before they sign up. Answer them before they leave the page.
