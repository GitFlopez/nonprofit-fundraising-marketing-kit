# Prompt 4 — Fundraising Event Marketing Kit

## Use This When
You're planning a gala, golf tournament, auction, walk/run, or any fundraising event that requires: pre-event promotion, sponsor acquisition, day-of scripts, and post-event donor conversion.

## What You'll Get
- Event concept brief (theme, audience, venue brief, sponsorship tiers)
- Save-the-date + formal invitation copy (mail + email versions)
- Sponsorship prospectus: 4 tiers with benefits matrix
- 6-email promotional sequence (announcement → follow-up → feature → last chance → day-of → post-event)
- Emcee / auctioneer script: welcome (3 min), mission moment (2 min), fund-a-need appeal (5 min, 3 emotional beats)
- Post-event donor conversion email (attendee → annual fund donor)
- Social media event recap kit (5 posts + 1 Instagram Story sequence)

---

## The Prompt

```

DELIVER:

## SECTION 1: EVENT CONCEPT BRIEF

One-page brief (300-350 words) covering:
- Theme statement: 2-3 sentences on the event's emotional narrative thread (what connects the dinner, the speaker, the appeal)
- Audience profile: who attends and what motivates their attendance (community connection, visibility, cause alignment)
- Venue brief: 3-4 sentence description suitable for vendor/venue proposals
- Sponsorship tier overview: summary of 4 tiers (full details in Section 3)
- Success metrics: what a "great event" looks like in numbers (revenue, donor conversion rate, new sponsors acquired)

## SECTION 2: INVITATIONS

**Save-the-Date (email version):**
- Subject: [3 options]
- Body: 100-125 words — event name, date, venue, who it benefits, "mark your calendar" CTA
- Include: ticket link placeholder and unsubscribe note

**Save-the-Date (print/mail version):**
- 50-75 word copy for a 4×6 or 5×7 card
- Headline, date, venue, "invitation to follow" line
- RSVP mechanism note

**Formal Invitation (email version):**
- Subject: [3 options]
- Body: 350-400 words
- Structure: honoree/event theme intro → mission impact moment → event details (what to expect) → ticket/table pricing → RSVP CTA
- P.S.: "Tables of 10 available — contact [NAME] for group reservations"

**Formal Invitation (print/mail version):**
- Inner envelope copy: 200-250 words, elegant but warm
- RSVP card copy: 75 words
- Outer envelope teaser copy: 15-20 words

## SECTION 4: 6-EMAIL PROMOTIONAL SEQUENCE

**Email 1 — Save the Date / Announcement (send 8-10 weeks out):**
Subject: [3 options]
Body: 200-250 words — launch the event, create anticipation, include early bird ticket link if applicable

**Email 2 — Sponsor Recruitment (send 7 weeks out, to prospects):**
Subject: [3 options]
Body: 250-300 words — sponsorship tiers, audience demographics, deadline, contact

**Email 3 — Honoree / Speaker Feature (send 5 weeks out):**
Subject: [3 options]
Body: 250-300 words — feature the honoree or program being spotlighted, connect it to the mission, include story element, ticket CTA

**Email 4 — Last Chance (send 2 weeks out):**
Subject: [3 options]
Body: 150-200 words — urgency (X seats remain), price or deadline reminder, simple CTA

**Email 5 — Day-Of Reminder (send morning of event):**
Subject: [3 options]
Body: 100-125 words — logistics reminder (time, parking, dress code), excitement building, see-you-tonight tone

**Email 6 — Post-Event Thank You + Giving Conversion (send within 48 hours):**
Subject: [3 options]
Body: 300-350 words
Structure: thank you for attending → event outcome (amount raised, impact statement) → for those who didn't attend: "You can still be part of what happened last night" → annual fund giving link
This email converts event attendees who didn't give at the event into annual fund donors — it's the most important email in the sequence.

## SECTION 5: EMCEE / AUCTIONEER SCRIPT

**A. Welcome Script (3 minutes / ~450 words)**
Structure:
- Opening line that acknowledges the room and sets the tone (not "good evening, it's so great to be here")
- Who's in the room and why they came (community recognition without being fawning)
- Brief mission statement — in plain language, not nonprofit-speak
- Program preview: what's happening tonight
- Housekeeping (phones, exits, timing) — brief and light

**B. Mission Moment / Beneficiary Story (2 minutes / ~300 words)**
Structure:
- Bridge from whatever preceded it ("Before we continue, I want to take two minutes...")
- Story: CARS format — context, action, result, so what
- Pause after the result — let it land
- Connect to the fund-a-need appeal: "What you're about to do in the next few minutes is write the next chapter of this story."

**C. Fund-a-Need / Paddle Raise Appeal (5 minutes / ~750 words)**
This is the highest-stakes 5 minutes of the year for your organization. Structure it in 3 emotional beats:

**Beat 1 — The Problem (90 seconds):**
- State the need in your community with one specific statistic
- Make it visceral but not hopeless
- "Tonight, in [CITY], [NUMBER] families..."

**Beat 2 — The Solution (90 seconds):**
- What your organization does
- Tie it to the dollar amounts you're about to ask for
- Pre-frame the gift tiers: "In a moment, I'm going to ask you to give at three levels. Here's what each level does."

**Beat 3 — The Ask (2 minutes):**
- Start at the highest level: "I need 5 people in this room to give $[HIGH AMOUNT]. Who will be first?" PAUSE. WAIT. Acknowledge.
- Move to mid level: "I need [NUMBER] people to give $[MID AMOUNT]." PAUSE.
- Move to entry level: "Everyone else — $[LOW AMOUNT] changes one [beneficiary]'s [situation]." PAUSE.
- Close: "If you've already given tonight — thank you. If you haven't, your paddle is in your seat. We're going to give you 60 seconds."
- Final close: announce total raised (once you have it) + thank you + transition back to program

**TIMING NOTE:** Include (PAUSE) stage directions throughout. Fund-a-need scripts read at 150 words/minute — this puts the 5-minute appeal at ~750 words. Time your emcee's run-through.
```

## Pro Tips

- **The fund-a-need pause is everything.** Most emcees rush through the silence after the ask. That silence is where the money lives. Train your emcee to be comfortable with 10-15 seconds of quiet after each ask.
- **Print the emcee script in 18pt font.** Emcees read at emotional moments under variable lighting. Small fonts create stumbles.
- **The post-event email is your most important conversion tool.** 30-40% of event-generated annual fund donors are converted in the 48 hours after the event — not at the event itself.
- **Sponsor prospectuses should include audience demographics.** Sponsors are buying access to your audience. Tell them who that audience is: household income, industry, community standing.
- **Email 3 (honoree feature) often has the highest open rate in the sequence** — people forward it to people who know the honoree. Use it to introduce new prospects to your mission.
