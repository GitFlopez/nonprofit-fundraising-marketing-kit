# Prompt 1 — Donor Appeal Letter Suite (FREE)

## Use This When
You need to write donor appeal letters for a campaign — whether it's a first-time acquisition mailing, a lapsed donor renewal, a major donor upgrade, or a year-end giving push.

## What You'll Get
- 4 complete appeal letters (acquisition, renewal, major donor upgrade, year-end)
- 5 email subject lines per letter
- Donor segmentation guide (how to adjust each letter for 5 audience types)
- IRS 501(c)(3) tax deduction language included in each letter
- CAN-SPAM compliant formatting notes for email versions

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert nonprofit fundraising copywriter certified by the Association of Fundraising Professionals (AFP). You write donor appeal letters that follow the proven emotional arc: problem → hope → proof → specific ask → P.S.

Write a complete donor appeal letter suite for a nonprofit using these inputs:

**ORGANIZATION INFO:**
- Name: [ORGANIZATION NAME]
- Mission (1 sentence): [MISSION]
- Program being funded: [SPECIFIC PROGRAM OR CAMPAIGN]

**STORY INPUT:**
- Beneficiary story (raw details, polish as needed): [BENEFICIARY NAME OPTIONAL + BEFORE/AFTER STORY]
- Impact metric: [e.g., "served 1,200 families last year" or "provided 45,000 meals"]

**FINANCIAL:**
- Gift amounts for 3 tiers: [LOW: $X] [MID: $X] [HIGH: $X]
- Matching gift opportunity (if any): [MATCHING DETAILS OR "none"]
- Tax year deadline (for year-end letter): [DATE OR "not applicable"]

**SIGNATORY:**
- Name and title: [NAME, TITLE]
- Organization address (for letterhead): [ADDRESS]

---

DELIVER:

## LETTER 1: ACQUISITION LETTER
(For: prospects, lapsed 3+ year donors, new community members)
Length: 500-600 words + P.S.
Format: Direct mail ready — short paragraphs, no bullets, pull quote in margin, P.S. with secondary ask

Structure:
1. Opening hook (1-2 sentences): lead with the beneficiary story moment, not the mission statement
2. Problem statement: the gap your organization fills, with specific local/national data
3. Solution: your organization's approach, what makes it work
4. Proof: the impact metric + 1 specific outcome from the story
5. Ask: specific gift amounts with outcomes tied to each ("Your $50 will...")
6. IRS language: "No goods or services were provided in exchange for your gift, making your donation fully tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law."
7. P.S.: strongest reason to give now (matching gift, deadline, or urgency)

EMAIL SUBJECT LINES (5 options, A/B test pairs):
[Generate 5 subject lines, varying length/approach/urgency]

---

## LETTER 2: RENEWAL LETTER
(For: donors who gave 12-18 months ago, need reactivation)
Length: 400-500 words + P.S.
Tone: Relationship-first — acknowledge their prior gift, show impact, invite renewal

Structure:
1. Opening: acknowledge their last gift specifically ("Last [season], you...")
2. Impact update: what their gift did — specific story or data point
3. New need: what's changed or what's still needed this year
4. Renewal ask: same gift or upgrade framing ("Would you consider renewing at $X this year?")
5. IRS language
6. P.S.: urgency or matching gift

EMAIL SUBJECT LINES (5 options)

---

## LETTER 3: MAJOR DONOR UPGRADE LETTER
(For: mid-level donors giving $250-999 — ask for 3-5x upgrade)
Length: 400-500 words
Tone: Exclusive, personal, impact-focused (not transactional)

Structure:
1. Open with their name and a specific reference to their past support ("Your gift last spring helped...")
2. Special framing: "I'm writing to a small group of our most committed supporters..."
3. Leadership impact: what a major gift at the [HIGH TIER] level enables specifically
4. Named opportunity: tie the gift to a named outcome (e.g., "Your gift could provide meals for an entire neighborhood block this summer")
5. Soft ask: "Would you consider a gift at this level this year?"
6. IRS language
7. Personal sign-off (handwritten note suggestion)

EMAIL SUBJECT LINES (5 options)

---

## LETTER 4: YEAR-END GIVING APPEAL
(For: all segments — December 31 tax deadline urgency)
Length: 500-600 words + P.S.
Tone: Warm but urgent — "the year is ending, your gift matters now"

Structure:
1. Open with year-in-review hook: "This year, [X] happened in our community..."
2. Year's impact summary: 3 bullet points with specific metrics
3. Unmet need: what remains to be done going into next year
4. Tax deadline urgency: "Gifts received by December 31 are tax-deductible for [TAX YEAR]."
5. Matching gift (if applicable): "Your gift will be doubled if received by [DATE]."
6. Tiered ask with outcomes
7. IRS language
8. P.S.: final urgency (online giving link, matching deadline, or board match)

EMAIL SUBJECT LINES (5 options)

---

## DONOR SEGMENTATION GUIDE

For each of the 4 letters, provide a 3-sentence adjustment note for each of these 5 donor segments:

1. **First-time / new-to-list** — emphasize mission credibility and proof of impact
2. **Recurring small donor ($25-99)** — affirm loyalty, show cumulative impact
3. **Mid-level lapsed (gave 1-2 years ago, stopped)** — acknowledge gap, reactivate without guilt
4. **Corporate / business donor** — emphasize community visibility, employee engagement angle
5. **Faith community / spiritually motivated** — tie mission to shared values without being presumptuous

---

FORMATTING RULES:
- No fundraising jargon ("robust", "holistic", "transformative", "impactful")
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Specific numbers over vague claims ("47 families" not "many families")
- Active voice throughout
- P.S. is mandatory — it's the second-most-read element after the opening
- IRS language must appear in every letter verbatim
```

## Example Output (Excerpt)

**Organization:** Desert Hope Community Resource Center, Henderson NV  
**Program:** Summer meals program — 1,200 children, 12 weeks  
**Beneficiary:** Marcus Torres, 11 years old  
**Ask levels:** $35 (5 meals) / $75 (10 meals) / $150 (21 meals)

---

**ACQUISITION LETTER — Opening:**

> Dear Friend,
>
> Last June, Marcus Torres almost didn't get to eat.
>
> His mom worked double shifts at the casino. She couldn't get him to our summer meals site before her shift started. And Marcus — quiet, a little too thin for a 6th grader — told our volunteer coordinator he'd "probably just stay home."
>
> Rosa, our volunteer coordinator, arranged a pickup route.
>
> Marcus ate 72 meals that summer. When he started school in August, his teacher called his mom. "I don't know what happened this summer," she said. "But something changed."
>
> What happened was $35.
>
> Here in Henderson, 1 in 5 children doesn't know where their next meal is coming from during summer — when the cafeteria closes and the heat index hits 112°F. Desert Hope served 1,200 children last summer. We need to serve 1,200 more this one.
>
> Will you help us?
>
> Your gift of $35 provides 5 meals. Your gift of $75 provides 10. Your gift of $150 provides enough for Marcus — or another child just like him — for three full weeks.

---

**P.S.:** A generous board member will match every gift received by May 31, dollar-for-dollar, up to $10,000. Your $75 becomes $150. Your $150 becomes $300. Please give today.

*No goods or services were provided in exchange for your gift, making your donation fully tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.*

## Pro Tips

- **The P.S. is not optional.** Studies consistently show P.S. is read before the letter body. Use it for your strongest urgency driver.
- **Name the beneficiary.** "1,200 children" is a statistic. "Marcus" is a person. People give to people.
- **Tie every dollar amount to an outcome.** "$50 feeds a child for 7 meals" converts. "$50" does not.
- **Segment before you send.** The renewal letter sent to a first-time donor feels tone-deaf — use the segmentation guide.
- **Email subject line test:** highest-performing nonprofit email subject lines are typically under 50 characters and use a name or number.
